Just another Inventory/Monitoring tool? Sure, BUT this time there's a twist. The backend is not a relational database, but it goes into the magic world of graphs. Using Neo4j as a back-end, the system should be able to manage a complex network structure without hassle and, in future, be able to use something like a reasoner to decide wether elements are connected, what the collective status is, how to optimize the monitoring operations.
The idea of this tool is to be a proof of concept and/or a prototype.
